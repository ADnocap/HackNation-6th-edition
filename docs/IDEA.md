# FINAL RECOMMENDATION — Challenge 02, The VC Brain

---

## A. THE CONCEPT

**Name:** **Counterproof**

**Pitch:** Every VC tool asks "who has already emitted a signal?" — which returns null for a first-time founder by construction. Counterproof asks "what evidence *should* exist if this claim were true?", searches for exactly that, and prices the gap — which is simultaneously a lie detector and a cold-start engine.

**Core insight, in three sentences.** For any claim — "€41K MRR", "500 users", "technical founder" — the system derives an *expected-evidence manifest* before it searches: the artifacts that would be observable if the claim were true, each with a findability prior conditioned on the founder's resource class (sector, geography, technical-vs-operator, company age, capital access). It then scores a likelihood ratio, `P(observed pattern | true) / P(observed pattern | false)`, so absence is evidence *only when absence was predicted to be unlikely*. Against a founder inflating a deck, missing-but-expected evidence is a refutation and the claim goes red; against a solo operator with no GitHub, the manifest itself predicts that absence, so it costs nothing and the interval stays *wide rather than low* — which is the anti-network-gate expressed as arithmetic instead of as a value statement.

**Resolving the judge split.** Judges 1 and 2 chose Counterproof; Judge 3 chose Residual. The disagreement is narrower than it looks, and both sides converged on the same merged system without noticing: Judge 1 grafted Residual's trademark filter *into* Counterproof, and Judge 3 grafted Cold Open's resource-class-conditioned manifest — which *is* Counterproof's core mechanism — into Residual. The two concepts differ on which mechanism is the *headline*, not on which mechanisms to build.

Judge 3's substantive claims are correct and are adopted in full: the pedigree-neutralization toggle is the most memorable four seconds available to anyone at this event, the empty-attorney-field trademark filter is the sharpest sourcing invention in the field, and leading the demo with a retrodiction backtest is a methodology detour that hands a judge an argument to chew on. All three are in the build below.

Judge 3's *structural* claim — build Residual as the base — is rejected for two reasons. First, Residual's differentiation is load-bearing on numbers that are circular in three independent places (R² regressed against a "conventional score" the team invents; conformal coverage calibrated against the same LLM judge with more context; an SPRT false-trigger rate that is an arithmetic consequence of a hand-set likelihood table). Every one of Judge 3's own critics flagged this, and for a submission whose credibility posture is quantified honesty, a self-referential headline statistic in front of a finance-native sponsor is a worse outcome than having no statistic. Second, Counterproof's core mechanism has **zero external dependencies** — a hand-authored prior table plus a logistic sum, buildable in an afternoon, with a fallback tier that works with the network down. In a 21-hour sprint, the concept whose differentiator cannot be killed by a rate limit, an auth wall, or a missing cohort is the correct base. Residual contributes its best mechanism (the toggle) and its best channel (the trademark filter); it does not contribute its architecture.

The final system therefore leads the *demo* with Residual's toggle and leads the *architecture* with Counterproof's manifest.

## B. WHY IT FITS THIS TEAM

**What carries real weight:**

- **Empirical-Bayes reference-class shrinkage (Efron–Morris partial pooling).** ~80 lines of numpy, and it is the only principled answer to cold start. The reference class is defined *only* by what was built and under what constraints — artifact type, sector, solo/team, resource tier — and contains no pedigree field. The shrinkage weight `k/(n+k)` becomes the cold-start UI: "71% of this number is your reference class, not you." It also delivers the brief's "never resets, sharpens with every milestone" as a mechanical consequence of `1/σ²` rising, not as a feature someone wrote.
- **Log-odds evidence accumulation with a published, hand-set reliability table.** Founder self-report −1.2, press +0.2, third-party observable +1.1, primary registry filing +2.4. Writing that table *honestly* and defending each row is a modelling skill; printing it on screen is what distinguishes it from an LLM emitting `0.87`.
- **Likelihood ratios for absence, with findability priors estimated from our own crawl.** `P(public GitHub | technical claim, this resource class)` is not invented — it is the empirical rate computed over the harvested population, with cell counts displayed and thin cells shrunk to the margin. Forty lines of pandas, and it is the difference between "we modelled absence" and "we guessed at absence."
- **Leave-one-evidence-out attribution.** Because the scorer is a closed-form normal-normal posterior in numpy rather than an LLM, dropping one evidence item and recomputing is exact and instantaneous. This is the FAQ's named highest-leverage stretch goal, implemented as *measurement* rather than as the model narrating itself — and the on-screen caption says exactly that.
- **k-sample self-consistency spread as the interval.** Cheap, honest, and it claims only what it does.
- **Days of Edge as date arithmetic.** Median lag between a channel signal firing and the person becoming consensus-visible. Immune to recognition leakage, needs no outcome labels, and produces the one sentence nobody else can say.
- **Point-in-time discipline as a read-path primitive.** Every read takes an `asof` and executes `WHERE observed_at <= :asof`. This is one wrapper function that *removes* work: trend stops being a separately-built feature and becomes "re-score at asof−90/−60/−30".

**Rejected as cosplay, deliberately and on the record:**

| Rejected | Why |
|---|---|
| Kelly / fractional-Kelly sizing | Check is fixed at $100K by the brief; log-utility on power-law payoffs at n=1 is mis-specified and a finance judge knows it |
| Mean-variance, efficient frontier, Monte Carlo fund returns | VC payoffs aren't Gaussian, variance isn't the risk, and it brushes explicitly out-of-scope fund ops |
| GARCH / copulas / regime-switching | No data feed, no visible output, pure vocabulary |
| Black-Litterman machinery | Keep the *idea* (thesis as prior); a weighted prior blend gets 95% and the machinery is invisible |
| **Benjamini–Hochberg FDR** | BH consumes p-values. We have LLM-derived log-odds and no null distribution. This is the single most catchable statistical error available, and it sits inside the trust layer |
| **Split/Mondrian conformal + coverage table** | Conformal needs a target variable. "Latent build capability" is never realized, so "79.4% at nominal 80%" would be coverage of our own model's other output — a checksum in a lab coat |
| **SPRT operating characteristic** | Keep the sequential trigger; delete "~3 false triggers per 100 founder-months." No null, no observation period, no earned number |
| **Thompson sampling over channels** | With zero funded outcomes every posterior is the prior; TS over pure priors is a random number generator with a citation |
| Inter-rater κ study | Blocks two people for 1.5h each at the worst hours to produce a number nobody asked for |
| Full Heckman correction | State the diagnosis in 20 seconds with the proposed IPW estimator; spend zero hours coding it |
| Any A100 fine-tuning | The round trip alone eats the sprint; everything here runs on a laptop in seconds |

The cut list is the credibility artifact. A finance-literate judge reading "we killed Kelly sizing because the check is fixed" learns more about the team than any model we could ship.

## C. THE SYSTEM

**Memory** is one append-only Postgres ledger. `observation(id, person_id, org_id, source, source_url, observed_at, ingested_at, claim_type, value, raw_excerpt, bbox, source_class, provenance_class, confidence)` plus `person`, `claim`, `evidence_link`, `founder_score_version`, `opportunity`, `stage_transition`, `axis_score`, `channel`, `excluded_source`. Nothing is updated or deleted; every score is a pure function of the ledger, which is why "never resets" is a schema property rather than a promise. Every read goes through **exactly one** function, `read_observations(asof, ...)` — and we grep for it on camera.

**Intelligence** is three separated layers with a hard boundary: the LLM *only* extracts and types observations and claims via strict JSON schema; all scoring is numpy over rows; all prose is Jinja-templated from the claim list. A claim with zero `evidence_ids` **physically cannot render as prose** — it renders as a gap row. Fabricated financials are a schema violation, not a prompt failure.

**Experience** is five views: Signal Feed (with the neutralization toggle and latency strip), Person page (Cold-Start Bench, manifest checklist, axes, score history), Claim list with the Receipt modal, Memo + typed decision card, and an Honesty panel. Every quant panel carries one plain-language line above it.

**The eight required elements:**

1. **Thesis Engine** — a persisted six-field JSON (sectors, stage, geography, check size, ownership target, risk appetite) that acts as hard filters and soft weights, with **risk appetite mapped to the maximum acceptable posterior interval width at which capital deploys** — so the thesis is load-bearing on our own core mechanic rather than a filter bar, and flipping it live re-ranks the board and moves one founder from "probe further" to "decide now."
2. **Smart data collection** — three live collectors plus a deck parser, all normalizing into the single append-only observation ledger with source class and provenance class, alongside a visible **"not collected, and why"** ledger naming LinkedIn headlines, GitHub stars and follower counts with the reason each was declined.
3. **Multi-attribute reasoning in one pass** — one structured LLM call parses the query into `{hard_filters, soft_weights}`, resolved by a single SQL filter plus rerank, with the parse rendered as chips; critically, constraints over *missing* attributes ("no prior VC backing") resolve as `P(satisfied | evidence)` rather than as hard filters, because a hard filter on absence deletes exactly the cold-start founders.
4. **Inbound Apply + fast Screen** — a literal two-field form (company name + deck) with the line "we didn't ask for team size, market or traction," feeding a cheap deterministic screen that kills clearly non-viable submissions with a visible reason and a "14 of 63 screened out" counter.
5. **Outbound Identify → Activate → Converge** — the trademark/HN/domain collectors identify; Activate drafts (never sends) a memo-before-contact message quoting the exact triggering observation with its date and URL and asking for counter-evidence to one specific claim; the response lands in the *same* `opportunity` row and the *same* screen queue as an inbound deck, shown side by side on camera.
6. **Three axes, never averaged** — Founder / Market (bullish-neutral-bear, categorical, so it structurally cannot be averaged) / Idea-vs-Market are three separate scorer objects with no composite column anywhere in the schema, ranked by Pareto non-dominated sorting over the 3-vector with a user-chosen sort axis, an "AXES DISAGREE" headline component, and typed `binding_axis` / `dissenting_axis` fields on the decision object.
7. **Per-claim Trust Score + evidence-backed memo** — trust is posterior log-odds per claim with four states including **ABSENT-BUT-EXPECTED**, displayed as a distribution ("6 verified / 5 unverified / 3 contradicted / 2 expected-but-absent") and never as a company-level mean; the memo ships exactly the five required sections plus a gaps block.
8. **Investor-grade UX** — the default card leads with three plain sentences and a stacked claim bar, with log-odds arithmetic, prior weights and the Pareto scatter one click down.

**Trend** is computed, never asserted: each axis re-scores at `asof−90/−60/−30/0` over the same ledger, the label is `improving`/`declining` only when the OLS slope's band excludes zero, and otherwise renders "insufficient dated observations (n=2)". Every point clicks through to its source row.

**On UCB/LCB routing:** we adopt the **lower-bound gate** (capital deploys only when the interval's lower bound clears the thesis bar, with the bar set by risk appetite) because it makes width cost money and nothing else. We **reject** upper-bound ranking for sourcing: in the cold-start cohort every interval is wide for the same reason, so ranking on the upper bound degenerates into ranking on ignorance, and the strict maximizer is a stranger with zero observations. Sourcing ranks on the Pareto frontier and the pedigree residual instead.

## D. THE SOURCING LAYER

**Design rule enforced in code:** a channel qualifies only if it can fire for a person with no GitHub, no funding and no network. GitHub-trending-by-stars is **banned as a primary channel** — ranking repos by stars is track-record sourcing by construction, i.e. rebuilding the exact network gate the challenge exists to replace. We say this on camera.

**Channel 1 — Self-filed trademark applications (the flagship).** Filtered to: filing basis **1(b) intent-to-use** (product not launched, therefore pre-fundraise by definition), TEAS Plus (cheapest tier), owner is an individual or an LLC under 180 days old, and **attorney field empty**. That last boolean does four jobs at once: someone is building, they have no law firm, therefore no funding, therefore no network. A trademark is ~$250 against a patent's $10K+, so it is the first-time unfunded founder's IP move, and it is typically filed at or before domain registration. The goods-and-services free text is a **machine-readable statement of what the product is** — we embed it and feed it directly into the Idea-vs-Market axis. Evertrace mines patents; nobody mines self-filed marks.

*Known coverage limit, stated on camera:* foreign-domiciled applicants have been required to appoint US counsel since 2019, so the empty-attorney marker selects for US-domiciled founders. Our outbound hero is therefore a US-domiciled solo operator, and we name the limitation rather than let a judge find it.

**Channel 2 — Hacker News via the Algolia API** (`hn.algolia.com/api/v1/search_by_date`, free, keyless, second-resolution unix timestamps, complete history). Not the front page — the low-visibility surface: first-ever Show HN posts from accounts under 90 days old, long technical answers in domain threads where the author has no other footprint, and "who wants to be hired" threads for operator history. We score the comment *text* for lived domain exposure, never the karma. One call returns a person, an utterance and a date — which is why it is the only collector that is genuinely trivial and genuinely cold-start-native.

**Channel 3 — Domain page reading, not domain registration.** Everyone tracks that a domain was registered; nobody fetches the page. For each candidate domain, `httpx` + regex + selectolax classify: live Stripe / Paystack / Lemon Squeezy checkout endpoint (**transacting today**), a pricing page, Calendly, a dated changelog, a waitlist form, or parked. Transacting-vs-parked is the highest-value bit in the entire domain signal and every vendor discards it. Twenty minutes of work.

**Everything else becomes a row in the "not collected, and why" ledger** — which scores better than a broken scraper and directly answers Area of Research 2.

**Channel scoring — the Days of Edge table.** One `GROUP BY` over `observed_at`: median lag between a channel signal firing and the person becoming consensus-visible (funding announcement, press, database entry). Date arithmetic, so it cannot leak through model recognition and needs no outcome labels. It produces the sentence no other submission can say: *"GitHub Trending: zero days of edge. It is beta. Everyone reads it. We defunded it."* Displayed with visible `n` and honest error bars — a channel at n=6 shows a wide bar and says so.

**Cold start — the founder with no GitHub, no funding, no network.** Four mechanisms, all visible in a named module:

1. **Reference-class prior.** `θ̂ = w·(direct evidence) + (1−w)·(reference-class posterior mean)`, `w = n/(n+k)`, where the class is `{artifact type, sector, solo/team, resource tier, region}` and contains **no pedigree field**. The UI states the split: "No track record found. Scoring on 4 proxy signals. Prior weight 71%. Interval [42, 79]. Here is what would narrow it."
2. **Absence priced by resource class — the load-bearing rule.** Missing expected evidence **widens the interval and never lowers the score**; absence that the findability prior predicted for this resource class is stamped *"not expected for this founder profile — not penalised"* and rendered as a grey chip. This single asymmetry is what stops the system re-encoding the network gate, and it is the one thing in the whole design that a competent generalist team will not converge on.
3. **Elicitation — we generate the evidence rather than retrieving it.** Value-of-Information picks the single claim whose resolution most reduces expected interval width, then emits **one** bespoke, falsifiable challenge that demands a *checkable pointer* rather than a story: not "tell us about traction" but "you say 40 paying users — name the channel that produced the first ten and paste one Stripe payout date we can verify." Responses are scored arithmetically on **verified-pointer yield** (pointers that survive Tavily verification ÷ pointers offered), **checkable-atom density** (named entities, dates, figures per 100 words via spaCy NER), and **concession** (does the answer volunteer a specific weakness — conceding scores *up*). Fluent, atom-free prose scores near zero. A polished ex-FAANG founder cannot beat this by pedigree; only someone who did the work produces atoms that corroborate. This is the one axis where a 24-hour team cannot lose to a $25K/yr vendor, because it cannot be won by data scale.
4. **Person spine with honest contact status.** A `person` table with deterministic blocking (normalized name + domain + handle) and a `contact_status ∈ {public_email, form_only, attorney_of_record, none}` rendered as a funnel counter: **"63 discovered · 21 contactable · 9 activated."** This converts our largest structural weakness into a self-reported one, which is the register the whole product trades in.

**The residual toggle.** One switch flips the leaderboard between raw and pedigree-neutralized (OLS on elite school, prior VC-backed employer, accelerator brand, follower count) and the ranking visibly reorders. **The R² number is deleted** unless externally anchored — the violent reorder carries 100% of the impact and the fabricated statistic adds only downside.

## E. THE DATA PLAN

Every observation row carries a `provenance_class` badge rendered in the UI, so a judge can filter to `live` only and tell real from authored instantly.

**Live, real, running on stage (~2.5h of one person's time).** HN Algolia pulls across the thesis sectors — free, no key, no rate pain, no HTML parsing. Domain page fetches with the transacting/parked classifier over every domain surfaced. Tavily for per-claim verification and Market-axis comparables. This is genuinely live and the sourcing board is not a fixture.

**Fixture, real, pre-downloaded (45 min).** **One day's USPTO trademark bulk XML, downloaded and committed to the repo in the first 90 minutes**, parsed offline, filtered to 1(b) + TEAS Plus + no attorney + individual/new-LLC owner. This is a deliberate decision made at hour zero, not a fallback discovered at hour eight: the USPTO Open Data Portal requires a registered key with identity verification that a Saturday cannot reliably deliver, and the daily XML DTD is an afternoon by itself. We keep 100% of the narrative — the filter, the $250 argument, the goods-and-services text feeding Idea-vs-Market — for a fraction of the cost, and nobody in a three-minute video can tell. Register the API key in the first fifteen minutes anyway; if it lands, promote the fixture to live.

**Synthetic, authored, ~90 minutes — and this is the highest-leverage 90 minutes in the build.** One inbound hero in a Maschmeyer sector (B2B fintech infra), 9-slide deck, with **four planted contradictions**: slide 7 claims €41K MRR against a public changelog cadence and review volume implying under 200 users; deck claims 12 employees against three traceable; founding date predates the first observable artifact by 14 months; a cited market comparable that raised at a different stage. Plus one deliberate expected-but-absent gap. Plus the **same person under a prior 2024 venture with a different name spelling**, carrying nine resolved claims, so entity resolution merges on camera and the Founder Score history spans two ventures.

**Critical detail: the contradiction fixtures are hosted at real public URLs** (a second static Vercel or GitHub Pages site — the fictional customer's newsroom, the changelog, the team page). The Validator performs a genuine HTTP GET against a genuine domain and genuinely fails to find the claim. If those fixtures are served from `localhost:3000/fixtures/`, the receipt — the single best moment in the demo — is indistinguishable from a mock. Twenty minutes to make the peak forensically real. Note also that "LinkedIn shows 3 employees" cannot be a fixture, because we declined LinkedIn in our own not-collected ledger; use a team page or job-board listing instead.

**Real people are pseudonymized on camera** (initials + channel + signals), the Refuter is disabled on real-person entities, outbound messages are **drafted and rendered but never sent**, and all three constraints are stated on screen. This is the correct posture for building scored dossiers on named private individuals from public records, and it reads as discipline rather than as limitation.

**The recognition probe survives as a standalone honesty artifact (30 min).** Take fifteen real 2021 pre-fame artifacts, redact and paraphrase, ask the model to name the company, report the leak rate. Then say on camera: *"We wanted to backtest against real outcomes. We ran the leakage probe first; the model identified nine of fifteen redacted artifacts. So we are not reporting a hit rate, because it would be memory, not skill. Here is the probe."* That volunteered non-result buys more with a VC than any hit rate we could have assembled.

## F. THE STACK

**Storage:** Supabase Postgres — the append-only ledger. Chosen because the Founder Score must *visibly persist and never reset*, and a hosted table browser we can show on camera makes that concrete. No pgvector (63 rows is a numpy matmul), no vector DB.

**Worker:** Python 3.11, run **locally**, writing into Supabase. `httpx` + `selectolax` (no Playwright — too fragile at 3am, and any source needing JS rendering gets cut rather than debugged), `pdfplumber` for deck text with coordinates, `PyMuPDF` for rendering slide-region crops as receipt images, `spaCy en_core_web_sm` for atom extraction. Modelling in `numpy` / `scipy` / `statsmodels` — the hierarchical posterior, OLS residualization, trend slopes with bands, leave-one-out attribution — roughly 250 lines total.

**Frontend:** Next.js on **Vercel**, Tailwind + shadcn primitives, Recharts. Reads Supabase directly. **One deploy target only** — the Vercel + separate FastAPI worker split is a CORS-and-env integration tax that always lands at 3am and produces zero demo pixels.

**LLM:** **OpenAI** with strict structured outputs for extraction, claim typing, atom extraction, challenge generation and the judge. `k=3` self-consistency on the four hero entities only. The LLM never emits a score and never writes prose.

**Search/verification:** **Tavily** — the Validator Agent's per-claim external checks, atom corroboration, and Market-axis comparable rounds. It sits on the critical path of the mechanism that *is* the product, which is the right way to use a sponsor tool.

**Demo safety:** `DEMO_MODE=true` is the **only** mode. Every LLM call, every Tavily response and every scraper result is disk-cached by content hash from hour three, and the frontend renders from a precomputed `demo.json`. Exactly one genuinely live call runs, early, on the sourcing board, where latency reads as authenticity rather than as a hang.

## H. THE DEMO

**0:00–0:20 — Open on the toggle.** No title card, product already moving. A live board of 63 founders surfaced this week. One switch: **RAW → PEDIGREE-NEUTRALIZED**. The list reorders violently; the ex-Stripe founder falls from #1 to #19; a solo US operator with no repo and no funding goes to #2. *"Every tool in this market ranks founders by how visible they are. Visibility is already priced. We rank on what's left. Forty-seven of these sixty-three have never met an investor."* No R². The reorder is the argument.

**0:20–0:55 — Where she came from, and the cold start.** Click through. Her file was opened by a **self-filed trademark application, intent-to-use, no attorney, filed eleven days ago.** Show the goods-and-services text: *"$250. It's the first-time founder's IP move — every vendor in this market mines patents, nobody mines this. And this sentence is a machine-readable description of the product, so it feeds the Idea-vs-Market axis directly."* Then the domain probe: a live Stripe endpoint. **She is transacting today.** The Cold-Start Bench opens: prior weight 71%, interval [42, 79], and the expected-evidence manifest with four grey rows — *"GitHub repo: not expected for this profile, P=0.11 from our own crawl. Absence costs her nothing. These widen the interval; they do not lower the score."*

**0:55–1:25 — Elicitation.** The interval is too wide to commit $100K. The system computes value of information, picks one claim, and generates a bespoke falsification challenge quoting her own sentence back with its date — then drafts the outreach, which we show and explicitly **do not send**. Her answer lands: three checkable atoms, two Tavily-corroborated live, one volunteered concession. The interval narrows on screen. Said flat: *"That evidence did not exist forty minutes ago. No database in this market has it. We generated it — and this magnitude is a hand-set prior, the mechanism is what we're claiming."*

**1:25–2:00 — THE MOMENT.** Switch to the inbound applicant: two fields, deck plus company name. *"We didn't ask for team size, market or traction."* The Validator streams: fourteen claims extracted, claim 7 turns red. Click it. **The receipt opens: on the left, the cropped slide bitmap reading "€41K MRR, June 2026"; on the right, the live-fetched page with its URL and fetch timestamp showing three employees and a silent changelog.** The log-odds panel shows the arithmetic: −1.2 self-report prior, −2.8 direct contradiction, −1.3 expected-and-absent. And then, between them, **his persistent credibility score — carried from a different company in 2024 where eight of nine claims verified — flips the claim to CONTRADICTED and the score's interval shifts on a two-venture history chart.** *"This follows him. Not this company — him."*

That is the quiet moment: a lie caught on camera, with the paperwork, and a consequence that follows the person into whatever they do next. No other team can do it, because it requires per-claim provenance, a person-keyed persistent posterior, and a real HTTP fetch, simultaneously. *(Engineering note: a Beta/normal posterior update is undramatic by construction. Verify at hour 14 that the movement is visible on screen; if not, the beat is the **state change plus the interval shift**, not a number ticking down.)*

**2:00–2:30 — Trust and disagreement.** Memo renders from surviving claims only. Claim distribution: 6 verified / 5 unverified / 3 contradicted / 2 expected-but-absent. **"Cap table: not disclosed"** sits there unfabricated — *"the system does not know this, and the renderer physically cannot make it up."* Headline: **"AXES DISAGREE — Founder bullish, Market bear, Idea-vs-Market neutral. This is a pivot bet on the person. We don't average them and we don't resolve it."** Click any number → leave-one-evidence-out waterfall → raw artifact with timestamp. *"Measured deltas, not the model's story about itself."*

**2:30–3:00 — The decision and the clock.** Typed decision card: **CONDITIONAL $100K**, implied ownership against the configured thesis, binding axis Founder, dissenting axis Market, three conditions to close, next action with owner and deadline, kill criteria armed. ## J. THE THREE BIGGEST RISKS

**1. The flagship channel evaporates.**

*Early warning:* at 17:15 the hero JSON is not rendering end-to-end in every view with hardcoded numbers. Also: anyone says "I'll wire that view once the scorer is done."
*Fallback:* the architecture is already built to absorb this — one deploy target, worker runs locally, frontend is a **pure renderer over a precomputed `demo.json`**, so a backend bug at hour 20 cannot break the video. **3. Credibility collapse from one unearned number.**

B lost 80 minutes at hour 19 discovering this, then rewrote the right-hand pane as a direct fetch, which is what it should always have been.

**Hour-zero decision.** Split verification into two mechanisms at hour zero and never confuse them: the receipt's right pane is `httpx.get(url)` with the response body, final URL and fetch timestamp rendered — a *retrieval*, deterministic, offline-cacheable. Tavily is used only for claims about entities that already exist on the public web (market comparables, prior-round corroboration), where an index is the right tool. State this split on camera; it reads as rigor.

## 6. The trademark filter returned three rows

**What happened.** One day of USPTO bulk XML, intersected with 1(b) ∧ TEAS Plus ∧ no attorney ∧ individual-or-new-LLC owner ∧ a thesis sector ∧ a resolvable live domain, yielded three usable people, none in fintech, none transacting. The parser worked perfectly. The flagship channel became one hand-authored hero, and the "$250 poverty-of-access marker" argument — which is genuinely our best idea — was delivered over a table with `n=3` at the bottom.

**Hour-zero decision.** Download **seven days**, not one — same parser, same 45 minutes, 7× the rows — and commit them at hour 1. Write the pass condition as a number *before* parsing: **≥25 post-filter rows and ≥1 with a live checkout endpoint by 13:45**, else drop immediately to the hand-pulled CSV ladder without debugging. A stated numeric gate is the difference between a ladder and a sunk cost.

## 7. The findability priors had no cells

**What happened.** The reference class was `{artifact type, sector, solo/team, resource tier, region}` — roughly 144 cells over 63 observations. Every cell was n=0 or n=1, so every displayed prior shrank to the marginal rate. "GitHub repo: not expected for this profile, P=0.11" was, in fact, one global number wearing a conditional's clothes, and the whole cold-start argument rests on that conditioning existing.

**Hour-zero decision.** Collapse the reference class to **two dimensions** — technical-vs-operator × has-company-domain — declared at hour zero. Print the cell count next to every prior. When a cell is under n=8, the UI says *"marginal rate; class too thin (n=3)"*. Admitting the class is thin is more credible than a fabricated conditional and takes ten minutes to implement.

## 8. The two-venture merge couldn't merge

**What happened.** The fixture deliberately gives the same person a different name spelling across ventures, and the resolver is deterministic blocking on normalized name + domain + handle. Normalized-name blocking does not match across a spelling variant — that's the point of the variant. The merge was hardcoded, and when a judge asked how it generalizes, the honest answer was audible.

**Hour-zero decision.** Make the merge key the **shared handle or verified domain**, never the name. The differing spelling is the *display* cue that makes the merge feel impressive; the match reason rendered on camera is "same GitHub handle, same registrant email domain, first observed 2024-03-11." Design the fixture around the mechanism you actually have.

## 9. Three runtimes, and RLS ate the toggle's build slot

**What happened.** "One deploy target" was not true: local Python worker, hosted Supabase, hosted Next.js, plus a second static site for fixtures. The frontend was specified as both "reads Supabase directly" and "renders from a precomputed demo.json." At 01:50 C hit Supabase row-level security with the anon key and lost 95 minutes — exactly the slot allocated to the neutralization toggle. Clean-clone verification at 07:30 then failed twice on missing env vars.

**Hour-zero decision.** The frontend reads **`demo.json` only**, committed to the repo, regenerated by one `python export_demo.py`. Zero client-side Supabase, zero client env vars. Supabase remains the worker's store and the on-camera "append-only ledger" prop — showing the table browser is the demo, not the data path. Clean clone becomes `npm i && npm run dev`, which is the only version of that check that passes at 07:30.

## 10. "Live" and "DEMO_MODE is the only mode" were never reconciled

**What happened.** Everything cached from hour three, frontend renders precomputed JSON — so the sourcing board is a fixture, while the video says it isn't. The one live call couldn't be reconciled with the precomputed 63 and was cut at hour 19. A judge who cloned the repo could see this.

**Hour-zero decision.** Decide at hour zero that live-ness is demonstrated by **insertion, not re-render**: an "ingest now" button runs one HN Algolia query and *appends* new rows to the board, visibly distinct from the precomputed set. Latency then reads as authenticity, failure degrades to "nothing appended," and the claim survives a clone.

## 11. Caching started at hour three; the $50 of credits did not survive to hour 18

**What happened.** Three hours of uncached extraction calls, plus `k=3` self-consistency creeping from 4 hero entities to the whole board when someone wanted axis scores everywhere. Rate-limited at 18:40.

**Hour-zero decision.** Content-hash disk cache is the **first file written**, before any collector. LLM calls are hard-capped to the four hero entities by a constant at the top of the module; the other 59 get deterministic extraction only, and the UI labels them so.

## 12. Nobody slept, and the honesty audit was rubber-stamped at 3am

**What happened.** The `n=` badge audit is the designated defence against the plan's own #1 risk, and it was scheduled at 03:15 — the worst cognitive hour available — and took nine minutes. Four unearned numbers shipped.

**Hour-zero decision.** Staggered 3-hour sleep rotation, agreed at hour zero, non-negotiable. Move the `n=` audit to **hour 12**, run it as a `grep` over the rendered JSX for digit patterns, and make the rule mechanical: a number without a sibling `n=` badge is deleted, not discussed.
